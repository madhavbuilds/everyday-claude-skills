# Insurance Reference

## UK Insurance Complaints

**Regulatory body:** Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)
**Ombudsman:** Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) — free to consumers
**FOS time limit:** Within 6 months of final response letter from insurer; generally within 6 years of the event

**Internal complaint process:**
1. Formal written complaint to insurer
2. They must acknowledge within 5 working days
3. Final response required within 8 weeks
4. After 8 weeks (or unsatisfied with response) → FOS

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally complain about the handling/rejection of my insurance claim under policy number [POLICY NO]. I believe [insurer name] has acted in breach of its obligations under the policy and FCA principles for businesses."

## Common Grounds for Insurance Disputes

### Claim Rejection
- Non-disclosure: "You didn't tell us about X" — challenge if not asked, not material, or innocent mistake
- Policy exclusion applied: challenge if exclusion wasn't clearly communicated at purchase (Consumer Insurance Act 2012)
- Contributory negligence: insurer says your actions contributed — challenge the degree
- Pre-existing condition: challenge if condition wasn't related to the claim

### Claim Underpayment
- Market value vs replacement value dispute
- Labour costs underestimated
- Like-for-like replacement not offered

### Policy Cancellation
- Mid-term cancellation without valid reason
- Renewal refused with no reason

### Key Law (UK):
- Consumer Insurance (Disclosure and Representations) Act 2012: You must take reasonable care not to make a misrepresentation, but insurer can't void for innocent non-disclosure
- Insurance Act 2015 (commercial): Proportionate remedies — insurer can't void entire policy for careless breach if remedy should be proportionate

**Template for disputed rejection:**
> "I am writing to formally appeal the rejection of my claim dated [DATE] under policy [NUMBER]. The claim was rejected on the basis of [stated reason]. I believe this rejection is incorrect/disproportionate for the following reasons: [specific reasons citing policy wording and/or relevant law]."

## US Insurance Claims

**State regulation:** Insurance is state-regulated in the US — each state has its own Insurance Commissioner
**Bad faith:** Insurers have a duty of good faith; denial of valid claims can give rise to bad faith claims (punitive damages possible)

**Common grounds to appeal:**
- Claim denied without adequate investigation
- Delay in claim processing
- Lowball settlement offer
- Policy exclusion applied incorrectly

**Escalation:** Internal appeal → State Insurance Commissioner complaint → Attorney (bad faith claim)

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally appeal the denial of my insurance claim [CLAIM NUMBER]. I believe this denial is improper under the terms of my policy [POLICY NUMBER] and applicable state insurance law."

## Health Insurance (US)

**Key law:** Affordable Care Act (ACA), ERISA (employer plans), state laws

**Appeal rights:**
- Internal appeal: must be allowed within 180 days of denial
- External review: independent third party (for most plans)
- Expedited appeal for urgent/ongoing care

**Grounds to appeal:**
- Medical necessity determination
- Out-of-network emergency care (surprise billing protections)
- Experimental treatment exclusion (if evidence supports)
- Pre-authorization denial

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally appeal the denial of coverage for [service/treatment] as set forth in your Explanation of Benefits dated [DATE], Claim Number [NUMBER]. I believe this service is a covered benefit under my plan and medically necessary as documented by my treating physician."

## Home Insurance Claims

**Contents vs buildings:** Make clear which policy applies
**New for old vs indemnity:** New for old replaces; indemnity deducts wear and tear — check your policy type
**Single article limit:** Many policies cap individual items (jewellery, electronics) — must be specified for higher value items
**Escape of water:** One of the most disputed — photograph all damage, get 2-3 repair quotes

**Evidence to gather:**
- Photos of damage (timestamp on)
- Purchase receipts or valuations for claimed items
- Repair quotes from 2-3 contractors
- Any correspondence with loss adjuster
