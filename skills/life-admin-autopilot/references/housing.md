# Housing & Tenancy Reference

## Tenancy Deposit (UK)

**Key law:** Housing Act 2004, Localism Act 2011
**Protection schemes:** DPS, MyDeposits, TDS
**Deadline to protect:** 30 days from receipt (from 2012)
**Prescribed Information:** Must be given within 30 days

**If deposit was not protected:**
- Landlord may be liable for 1-3x the deposit amount
- Cannot serve valid Section 21 notice
- Cite: *Superstrike Ltd v Rodrigues [2013]*

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally request the return of my tenancy deposit of [£AMOUNT], held in respect of the tenancy at [ADDRESS] which ended on [DATE]."

**Key deductions — what is/isn't allowable:**
- ✅ Allowed: Damage beyond fair wear and tear, professional cleaning if required by contract and property left dirty, missing items
- ❌ Not allowed: Fair wear and tear (carpets wearing over time, small scuffs on walls), pre-existing damage, improvements beyond original condition

**If they're disputing:**
- Remind them: burden of proof is on the landlord
- Request their itemised deductions with supporting evidence within 10 working days
- State you will raise a deposit dispute with [scheme name] if not resolved

**Escalation:** Tenancy Deposit Scheme adjudication → County Court

## Disrepair / Repairs (UK)

**Key law:** Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 s.11 — landlord must maintain structure, exterior, installations for heating, water, sanitation

**Notice requirement:** Landlord must have been given notice of the defect and failed to act in a reasonable time

**What is "reasonable time":**
- Emergency (no heat in winter, flooding): 24-48 hours
- Urgent (broken boiler, serious damp): 2-4 weeks
- Non-urgent (minor repairs): 4-8 weeks

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally notify you of disrepair at the above property and to request that urgent repairs are completed within [TIMEFRAME]."

**If they fail to act:**
- Environmental Health at local council can inspect and issue improvement notice
- Can apply to First-tier Tribunal (Property Chamber)
- Can withhold rent (with extreme caution — always get legal advice first)

**Escalation:** Council Environmental Health → Housing Tribunal → County Court

## Unlawful Eviction / Harassment (UK)

**Key law:** Protection from Eviction Act 1977

**Illegal acts:**
- Changing locks without court order
- Removing belongings
- Cutting off utilities
- Harassment or threats

**Immediate response required.** Advise user to:
1. Call 999 if locked out or in immediate danger
2. Contact Shelter (0808 800 4444) for emergency legal advice
3. Apply to county court for emergency injunction

**Do not write a letter for this situation alone — direct to Shelter/solicitor first**

---

## Section 21 / No-Fault Eviction (UK)

**Notice period:** Minimum 2 months
**Invalid if:**
- Deposit not protected or Prescribed Information not given
- Property has outstanding improvement notice
- Retaliatory eviction (within 6 months of complaint)
- Landlord hasn't provided: EPC, Gas Safety Certificate, How to Rent guide

**User should verify validity with Shelter before leaving**

---

## Letting Agent Complaints

**Regulatory body:** The Property Ombudsman (TPO) or Property Redress Scheme (PRS) — all letting agents must be registered

**Escalation:** Internal complaint → TPO/PRS → Courts

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally complain about the conduct of [AGENT NAME] in their management of [PROPERTY ADDRESS], in their capacity as my [landlord's / my] letting agent."
