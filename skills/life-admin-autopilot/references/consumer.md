# Consumer Rights Reference

## UK Consumer Rights

**Key law:** Consumer Rights Act 2015 (goods + services + digital)

### Goods Must Be:
- Of satisfactory quality
- Fit for purpose
- As described

### Your Rights Timeline:
| Period | Right |
|---|---|
| 0–30 days | Full refund (short-term right to reject) |
| 30 days – 6 months | Repair or replacement first; refund if that fails |
| 6 months – 6 years | You must prove the fault existed at point of sale |

### Services Must Be:
- Carried out with reasonable care and skill
- Within a reasonable time (if no time was agreed)
- At a reasonable price (if no price was agreed)

**Template opener (faulty goods):**
> "I am writing under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 to request [a full refund / repair / replacement] in respect of [ITEM] purchased on [DATE] for £[AMOUNT]. The item has failed to meet the standard of satisfactory quality as it [DESCRIPTION OF FAULT]."

## Subscription Traps / Continuous Payment Authority

**What it is:** Company continues charging after cancellation, or charges after free trial without adequate notice.

**Your right:** You can instruct your bank to cancel a Continuous Payment Authority at any time, regardless of what the company says.

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to request the immediate cancellation of the recurring payment / subscription associated with my account, and to request a refund of charges made after [DATE OF CANCELLATION REQUEST]."

**Bank instruction template:**
> "I am writing to instruct you to cancel the Continuous Payment Authority to [COMPANY] under reference [if known], with immediate effect. Please also dispute charges made after [DATE] under this authority."

## Package Holiday / Travel (UK)

**Key law:** Package Travel and Linked Travel Arrangements Regulations 2018

**Rights:**
- Full refund if holiday significantly changed before departure
- Compensation for significant problems during holiday
- 14-day statutory cooling off for most online bookings (not always flights)

**Escalation:** ABTA (travel agents/tour operators) → ATOL (flight packages) → County Court

---

## Second-hand Goods

- Consumer Rights Act still applies to goods bought from a trader
- Private sales: lower protection (misrepresentation law still applies)
- Online marketplace: platform may have buyer protection

---

## Mis-selling (Financial Products)

**Key law:** Financial Services and Markets Act 2000, FCA rules

**Common types:** PPI, unsuitable investments, undisclosed commission on loans/mortgages

**Escalation:** Internal complaint → Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS) within 6 years of purchase or 3 years of awareness

---

## Cooling Off Rights (Distance Selling)

**Key law:** Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013

**Your right:** 14 calendar days to cancel any distance sale (online, phone, door-to-door)

**Exceptions:** Perishable goods, personalised items, digital content once download starts (if you consented to immediate delivery)

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to exercise my right to cancel under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013. I purchased [ITEM/SERVICE] on [DATE] (Order Reference: [REF]) and I wish to cancel within the 14-day cooling off period."

---

## Chargebacks (Bank Card)

**What it is:** Disputing a card transaction with your bank
**When to use:** Goods not received, service not provided, company gone bust, fraud
**How:** Contact your bank directly and cite "chargeback" or Section 75 (for credit cards over £100 under Consumer Credit Act 1974)

**Section 75 (Credit Cards only, UK):**
> "I am writing to make a claim under Section 75 of the Consumer Credit Act 1974. I used my [BANK NAME] credit card to purchase [ITEM] from [COMPANY] on [DATE] for £[AMOUNT]. The [goods were not delivered / service was not provided / goods are faulty] and the retailer has refused to provide a remedy."

**Escalation:** Bank complaint → Financial Ombudsman Service
