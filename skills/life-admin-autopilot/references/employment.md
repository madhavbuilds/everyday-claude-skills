# Employment Reference

## Unfair Dismissal (UK)

**Key law:** Employment Rights Act 1996
**Qualifying period:** 2 years continuous employment (with some exceptions)
**Time limit:** 3 months less one day from effective date of termination to file ET1

**Fair reasons for dismissal (s.98 ERA 1996):**
- Capability or qualifications
- Conduct
- Redundancy
- Statutory illegality
- Some other substantial reason (SOSR)

**Automatically unfair dismissal (no qualifying period needed):**
- Whistleblowing (Public Interest Disclosure Act 1998)
- Pregnancy or maternity leave
- Trade union membership/activities
- Asserting a statutory right
- Health and safety activities

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally raise a grievance / appeal my dismissal under the Employment Rights Act 1996. My effective date of termination was [DATE] and I believe my dismissal was unfair for the following reasons..."

**Escalation:** Internal grievance/appeal → ACAS Early Conciliation (mandatory before ET) → Employment Tribunal

## Unpaid Wages / Holiday Pay (UK)

**Key law:** Employment Rights Act 1996, Working Time Regulations 1998
**Time limit:** 2 years backdated unlawful deductions claim (ET); 6 years in civil courts

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally request payment of [£AMOUNT] in [wages/holiday pay] which remain outstanding from my employment. This constitutes an unlawful deduction from wages under the Employment Rights Act 1996."

**Escalation:** Internal → ACAS → Employment Tribunal (unlawful deduction) OR County Court (breach of contract)

## Disciplinary / Grievance (UK)

**ACAS Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures** — must be followed or tribunal can adjust award by up to 25%

**Grievance steps:**
1. Put grievance in writing to employer
2. Attend meeting (right to be accompanied by colleague or trade union rep)
3. Appeal if outcome unsatisfactory

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to raise a formal grievance in accordance with your grievance policy and the ACAS Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures."

---

## Redundancy (UK)

**Statutory redundancy pay:** Based on age, length of service, weekly pay (capped at £643/week for 2024/25)
- Age under 22: 0.5 weeks per year
- Age 22–40: 1 week per year  
- Age 41+: 1.5 weeks per year

**Selection criteria:** Must be objective and non-discriminatory (LIFO acceptable but risky; skills/performance preferred)

**Consultation:** Minimum 30 days for 20-99 redundancies; 45 days for 100+

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to raise concerns about the redundancy process and/or to appeal my selection for redundancy. I believe the selection process was [unfair/discriminatory/procedurally flawed] for the following reasons..."

## Discrimination (UK)

**Key law:** Equality Act 2010
**Protected characteristics:** Age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership, pregnancy/maternity, race, religion/belief, sex, sexual orientation

**Types:**
- Direct discrimination: treated less favourably because of characteristic
- Indirect discrimination: provision/criterion/practice that disadvantages group
- Harassment: unwanted conduct related to characteristic
- Victimisation: treated badly for making/supporting a complaint

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally raise a complaint of [direct discrimination/harassment/indirect discrimination] under the Equality Act 2010. I believe I have been treated less favourably on the grounds of my [protected characteristic]."

**Escalation:** Internal grievance → ACAS → Employment Tribunal (3 months less one day from act complained of)

## US Employment Reference

**At-Will employment:** Most US states allow termination without cause (exceptions: Montana, implied contract, public policy exceptions)

**Federal protections:**
- Title VII (Civil Rights Act): race, color, religion, sex, national origin
- ADA: disability
- ADEA: age (40+)
- FMLA: family/medical leave (50+ employee companies)
- FLSA: minimum wage, overtime

**Wrongful termination grounds:**
- Discrimination (protected class)
- Retaliation for protected activity (whistleblowing, workers comp, OSHA complaint)
- Breach of implied contract (employee handbook, verbal promises)
- Violation of public policy

**Escalation:** HR complaint → EEOC charge (required before federal discrimination lawsuit, 180/300 day deadline) → State agency → Federal/state court

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally raise a complaint regarding [discriminatory treatment/retaliation/wrongful termination] in violation of [Title VII/ADA/ADEA/applicable state law]. I am preserving my right to file a charge with the EEOC."
