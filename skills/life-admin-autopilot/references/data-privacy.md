# Data Privacy & GDPR Reference

## Subject Access Request (SAR) — UK/EU

**Legal basis:** UK GDPR Article 15 / EU GDPR Article 15
**Response deadline:** 1 calendar month (can extend by 2 months for complex/numerous requests — they must tell you within 1 month)
**Cost:** Free (in most cases)

**What you can request:**
- All personal data held about you
- Purposes of processing
- Categories of data
- Recipients or categories of recipients
- How long data will be retained
- Source of data (where not collected from you)
- Any automated decision-making / profiling logic

**Template:**
---
Subject: Subject Access Request — [YOUR FULL NAME] — [DATE OF BIRTH if needed for identification]

Dear Data Protection Officer / Data Controller,

I am writing to make a Subject Access Request under Article 15 of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR).

Please provide me with all personal data you hold about me, including but not limited to:
- All records, files, notes, and communications that contain my personal data
- Details of the purposes for which my data is processed
- Details of any third parties to whom my data has been disclosed
- The retention periods applied to my personal data
- Any automated decision-making that has been applied to my data

For identification purposes I provide: [Full name, address, date of birth, account number/customer reference if applicable].

If you require any further information to process this request, please contact me within one calendar month. I expect to receive the requested information within one calendar month of receipt of this letter, in accordance with your statutory obligations.

Yours sincerely,
[NAME]
---

**Escalation:** ICO complaint (Information Commissioner's Office) at ico.org.uk → Courts

## Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") — UK/EU

**Legal basis:** UK GDPR Article 17
**When it applies:**
- Data no longer necessary for original purpose
- You withdraw consent (where consent was the legal basis)
- You object and there are no overriding legitimate grounds
- Data processed unlawfully

**Does NOT apply when:**
- Data is needed to comply with legal obligation
- Data is needed for legal claims
- Freedom of expression/information interests apply
- Public interest in scientific/historical research

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to exercise my right to erasure of my personal data under Article 17 of the UK GDPR. I request that you erase all personal data you hold about me, and confirm in writing that this has been done within 30 days."

## Data Breach Notification to ICO (UK)

**When to report:** If you believe an organisation has suffered a data breach affecting your data
**Where:** ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/

**What to include in your complaint letter to the organisation:**
- When you became aware of the breach
- What data was affected
- What harm has occurred or may occur
- What remediation you expect

## CCPA — California Consumer Privacy Act (USA)

**Rights include:**
- Right to know what personal information is collected
- Right to delete personal information
- Right to opt-out of sale of personal information
- Right to non-discrimination for exercising rights

**Response deadline:** 45 days (extendable by another 45 with notice)

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to exercise my rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). Please provide me with [a complete disclosure of / deletion of] all personal information you have collected about me."

---

## Marketing Opt-Out / Unsubscribe

**UK law:** PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations)
**US law:** CAN-SPAM Act

**Template opener:**
> "I am writing to formally request that you immediately cease all direct marketing communications to me by [email/post/phone/SMS]. Please confirm receipt and compliance within 14 days."

**Escalation (UK):** ICO → Courts
