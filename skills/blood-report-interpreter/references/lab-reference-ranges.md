## Complete Blood Count (CBC)

| Marker | Standard Range (Adult) | Optimal Sub-range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| WBC | 4.5–11.0 × 10³/µL | 5.0–8.0 | Very high may suggest infection/inflammation; very low may suggest bone marrow or immune issues |
| RBC (Male) | 4.7–6.1 × 10⁶/µL | 5.0–5.5 | |
| RBC (Female) | 4.2–5.4 × 10⁶/µL | 4.5–5.0 | |
| Hemoglobin (Male) | 13.5–17.5 g/dL | 14.5–16.5 | Below 13 = anemia in males |
| Hemoglobin (Female) | 12.0–15.5 g/dL | 13.0–15.0 | Below 12 = anemia in females |
| Hematocrit (Male) | 41–53% | 43–50% | |
| Hematocrit (Female) | 36–46% | 38–44% | |
| MCV | 80–100 fL | 85–95 | Low = microcytic (iron, thal); High = macrocytic (B12, folate, alcohol) |
| MCH | 27–33 pg | 28–32 | |
| MCHC | 32–36 g/dL | 33–35 | |
| RDW | 11.5–14.5% | <13.0 | High RDW = variation in red cell size; suggests mixed deficiency or early deficiency |
| Platelets | 150,000–400,000/µL | 200,000–350,000 | <100k = thrombocytopenia; >450k = thrombocytosis |
| Neutrophils | 1.8–7.7 × 10³/µL | 2.5–6.0 | High in bacterial infection; low in viral, autoimmune, drug effects |
| Lymphocytes | 1.0–4.8 × 10³/µL | 1.5–3.5 | |
| Monocytes | 0.2–0.9 × 10³/µL | 0.2–0.6 | |
| Eosinophils | 0.0–0.5 × 10³/µL | <0.3 | High in allergies, parasites, some autoimmune |
| Basophils | 0.0–0.1 × 10³/µL | <0.1 | |

## Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)

| Marker | Standard Range | Optimal | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Glucose (Fasting) | 70–99 mg/dL | 75–90 | 100-125 = prediabetes; ≥126 = diabetes (fasting) |
| BUN | 7–25 mg/dL | 10–18 | Elevated: dehydration, high protein, kidney issues |
| Creatinine (Male) | 0.7–1.3 mg/dL | 0.8–1.1 | |
| Creatinine (Female) | 0.5–1.1 mg/dL | 0.6–0.9 | |
| eGFR | >60 mL/min/1.73m² | >90 | 60-89 = mildly reduced; 30-59 = moderately reduced; <30 = severely reduced |
| BUN/Creatinine Ratio | 10–20 | 12–18 | High = dehydration or GI bleed; Low = liver disease, malnutrition |
| Sodium | 136–145 mEq/L | 138–142 | |
| Potassium | 3.5–5.0 mEq/L | 4.0–4.5 | Critical: <2.5 or >6.0 requires prompt evaluation |
| Chloride | 96–106 mEq/L | 99–104 | |
| CO2 (Bicarbonate) | 22–29 mEq/L | 24–27 | |
| Calcium | 8.5–10.5 mg/dL | 9.0–9.9 | High: hyperparathyroidism, malignancy; Low: hypoparathyroidism, Vit D deficiency |
| Total Protein | 6.0–8.3 g/dL | 6.8–7.8 | |
| Albumin | 3.5–5.0 g/dL | 4.0–5.0 | Low in malnutrition, liver disease, chronic inflammation |
| Globulin | 2.0–3.5 g/dL | 2.0–3.0 | |
| A/G Ratio | 1.2–2.2 | 1.5–2.0 | |
| Total Bilirubin | 0.2–1.2 mg/dL | <0.9 | High: liver disease, hemolysis, Gilbert's syndrome |
| Direct Bilirubin | 0.0–0.3 mg/dL | <0.2 | |
| ALT | 7–56 IU/L | <30 | Liver-specific enzyme; elevated in liver damage |
| AST | 10–40 IU/L | <30 | Less liver-specific; also in muscle, heart |
| ALP | 44–147 IU/L | <100 | Elevated in liver/bone disease; high in children/teens (normal) |
| GGT | Male: <50 IU/L, Female: <30 IU/L | <25 | Sensitive marker for alcohol use, fatty liver, bile duct disease |

## Lipid Panel

| Marker | Standard Range | Optimal | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total Cholesterol | <200 mg/dL | <180 | >240 = high risk; context-dependent with HDL |
| LDL | <130 mg/dL | <100 | <70 for high-risk patients; context is everything |
| HDL (Male) | >40 mg/dL | >60 | Higher is generally better; protective |
| HDL (Female) | >50 mg/dL | >60 | |
| Triglycerides | <150 mg/dL | <100 | 150-199 = borderline high; >500 = pancreatitis risk |
| Non-HDL Cholesterol | <160 mg/dL | <130 | Better predictor than LDL alone |
| Total/HDL Ratio | <5.0 | <3.5 | Lower = better cardiovascular risk profile |

## Thyroid Panel

| Marker | Standard Range | Optimal | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| TSH | 0.4–4.0 mIU/L | 1.0–2.5 | High = hypothyroid; Low = hyperthyroid; ranges are controversial |
| Free T4 | 0.8–1.8 ng/dL | 1.0–1.5 | Primary thyroid hormone; low with high TSH = hypothyroidism |
| Free T3 | 2.3–4.2 pg/mL | 3.0–4.0 | Active form; low despite normal T4 = conversion problem |
| Reverse T3 | 9.2–24.1 ng/dL | <15 | High rT3 can block T3 action; associated with stress, illness |
| Anti-TPO | <35 IU/mL | <10 | Positive = Hashimoto's autoimmune thyroiditis |
| Anti-Thyroglobulin | <40 IU/mL | <20 | Another Hashimoto's marker |
| TRAb | <1.0 IU/L | Negative | Graves' disease marker |

## Vitamins & Minerals

| Marker | Standard Range | Optimal | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vitamin D (25-OH) | 20–100 ng/mL | 40–60 | Many consider <30 deficient despite lab range; universal concern |
| Vitamin B12 | 200–900 pg/mL | 500–900 | Below 400 many practitioners consider functional deficiency |
| Folate (Serum) | >2.7 ng/mL | >10 | |
| Folate (RBC) | >140 ng/mL | >400 | Better long-term indicator than serum |
| Iron | 60–170 µg/dL | 80–130 | Variable by sex and lab |
| Ferritin (Male) | 20–250 ng/mL | 70–150 | Iron storage; low ferritin before low iron/hemoglobin |
| Ferritin (Female) | 12–150 ng/mL | 50–100 | Low ferritin is one of the most underdiagnosed conditions in women |
| TIBC | 250–370 µg/dL | 250–310 | High TIBC + low ferritin = iron deficiency |
| Transferrin Saturation | 20–50% | 25–40% | <20% = iron deficiency; >45% = iron overload |
| Magnesium (Serum) | 1.7–2.2 mg/dL | 2.0–2.2 | Serum levels often normal even when intracellular is low |
| RBC Magnesium | 4.2–6.8 mg/dL | 5.5–6.8 | Better indicator of cellular magnesium status |
| Zinc | 70–120 µg/dL | 90–110 | |
| Copper | 70–140 µg/dL | 80–110 | Zinc and copper compete; balance matters |
| Selenium | 70–150 ng/mL | 100–140 | Important for thyroid hormone conversion and immune function |

## Diabetes & Metabolic Markers

| Marker | Standard Range | Optimal | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| HbA1c | <5.7% | <5.3% | 5.7–6.4% = prediabetes; ≥6.5% = diabetes; reflects 3-month average glucose |
| Fasting Insulin | 2–25 µIU/mL | 2–8 | High fasting insulin precedes high glucose by years; key early warning |
| HOMA-IR | <2.0 | <1.5 | Insulin resistance index; calculated as (fasting glucose × fasting insulin) / 405 |
| C-Peptide | 0.8–3.5 ng/mL | 1.0–2.5 | Indicates insulin production capacity |

## Inflammation & Cardiovascular Risk

| Marker | Standard Range | Optimal | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| hsCRP | <3.0 mg/L | <1.0 | <1.0 = low CV risk; 1-3 = intermediate; >3 = high risk |
| ESR | Male: <15 mm/hr, Female: <20 mm/hr | <10 | Nonspecific; elevated in infection, autoimmune, malignancy |
| Homocysteine | 4–15 µmol/L | <10 | High associated with CV risk and B vitamin deficiency |
| Lipoprotein(a) | <30 mg/dL | <20 | Genetic CV risk marker; does not respond to statins |
| ApoB | <100 mg/dL | <80 | Better LDL particle measure; increasingly preferred |

## Urinalysis Key Markers

| Marker | Normal | Abnormal | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protein | Negative | Trace/Positive | Persistent proteinuria = kidney disease |
| Glucose | Negative | Positive | Suggests diabetes or renal glycosuria |
| Ketones | Negative | Positive | Fasting, DKA, low-carb diet |
| Blood | Negative | Positive | UTI, kidney stones, cancer (requires follow-up) |

| Nitrites | Negative | Positive | Bacterial infection (gram-negative) |

## Drug-Marker Interactions Reference

Common medications that affect lab values:

| Drug/Class | Markers Affected | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Statins | CK, ALT, AST | ↑ (muscle damage signal) |
| Metformin | B12, MCV | ↓ B12 (can cause macrocytic anemia) |
| PPIs (omeprazole, etc.) | Magnesium, B12, Iron | ↓ (impairs absorption) |
| Thyroid hormone (levothyroxine) | TSH, Free T4 | ↓ TSH, ↑ T4 (dose-dependent) |
| Oral Contraceptives | CRP, Ferritin, Copper, SHBG | ↑ CRP, ferritin, copper, SHBG |
| Biotin supplements | TSH, T4, hCG, Troponin | Can falsely alter immunoassay tests — instruct patient to stop 48h before labs |
| NSAIDs | Creatinine, Potassium, eGFR | ↑ creatinine, ↑ potassium, ↓ eGFR |
| Corticosteroids | Glucose, WBC, Eosinophils | ↑ glucose, ↑ WBC, ↓ eosinophils |
| Alcohol | GGT, AST, MCV, Triglycerides, Uric Acid | ↑ all |
| Diuretics | Potassium, Sodium, Creatinine | Variable direction by type |
